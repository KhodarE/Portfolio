Rivest–Shamir–Adleman or also known as RSA, is a encryption and decryption that uses asymmetric cryptography algorithm. It uses two different keys to encrypt and decrypt. The first key being the public key that is used to encrypt and the private key is used to decrypt the message to plaintext so it can be viewed by the recipent.


RSA is used in modern systems to protect individuals data on the internet and company systems. Encryption is needed in all systems as it provides the users of a system the assurance that their private information such as their details, passwords, and credit card information is encapsulated and protected from hackers and the general public from attaining access to the precious data.
